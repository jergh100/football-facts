Why Do Players Get Yellow Cards in Football?

A yellow card is a caution given by the referee for misconduct or unsporting behavior during a match.

Common reasons for receiving a yellow card include:

  • Unsporting behavior (diving, reckless tackles)
  • Dissent by word or action
  • Delaying the restart of play
  • Failing to respect the required distance on free kicks or corners
  • Entering or leaving the field without permission

Two yellow cards in a match lead to a red card and automatic ejection from the game.

Fun Fact: Yellow and red cards were introduced at the 1970 FIFA World Cup to provide a universal visual signal across languages.
