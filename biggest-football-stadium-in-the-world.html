Biggest Football Stadium in the World

The Rungrado 1st of May Stadium, located in Pyongyang, North Korea, is officially the biggest football stadium in the world by capacity.

It can reportedly hold up to 114,000 spectators, although exact figures are debated due to limited access and verification.

Other large stadiums include:

  • 🏟️ Camp Nou (Barcelona, Spain) – ~99,000
  • 🏟️ Melbourne Cricket Ground (used for football in Australia) – ~100,000
  • 🏟️ Wembley Stadium (London, England) – ~90,000
  • 🏟️ Salt Lake Stadium (India) – ~85,000

Fun Fact: Despite its massive size, the Rungrado Stadium is rarely full. Some of the world’s most passionate football atmospheres come from smaller venues!
