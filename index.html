How long is a football match?

A standard football match lasts 90 minutes, split into two 45-minute halves, plus a 15-minute halftime break. Additional stoppage time is added at the referee's discretion.

Fun Fact: The longest recorded professional match lasted over 3 hours due to multiple extra times and penalties!

Most goals in a Premier League season

Erling Haaland set the record with 36 goals in the 2022/23 Premier League season.

Historical Highlight: The previous record was held by Andy Cole and Alan Shearer with 34 goals each.

Fastest Premier League player

As of the latest data, Kyle Walker clocked the fastest top speed in a match: 37.31 km/h.

He was closely followed by Mudryk, Nunez, and Adama Traoré.

How much do Premier League referees get paid?

Premier League referees earn around £1,500 per match, with annual retainers between £38,000–£42,000.

Top-level international referees earn more when officiating UEFA or FIFA matches.
