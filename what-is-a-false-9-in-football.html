What Is a False 9 in Football?

A false 9 is a type of striker who drops deeper into midfield instead of staying up front like a traditional centre-forward.

🎯 How It Works

  • Pulls defenders out of position to create space behind
  • Links midfield and attack by creating overloads centrally
  • Relies on teammates (often wingers or attacking midfielders) to make runs behind

🔝 Famous False 9 Players

  • Lionel Messi – The classic false 9 during Pep Guardiola’s Barcelona era
  • Francesco Totti – Used the role at Roma long before it was a trend
  • Roberto Firmino – Modern example who drops deep to link play for Liverpool

Fun Fact: The term "false 9" became widely used after Guardiola’s Barcelona used Messi in that role against Real Madrid in 2009 — and won 6–2.
