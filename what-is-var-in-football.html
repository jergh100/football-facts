What is VAR in Football?

VAR stands for Video Assistant Referee, a technology used in football to help referees make more accurate decisions.

VAR reviews four key match-changing situations:

  • Goals – Offsides, handballs, or fouls in the buildup
  • Penalty decisions
  • Direct red card incidents
  • Mistaken identity for cards

The on-field referee can either accept the VAR’s input or review footage on a pitch-side monitor before making a final decision.

Fun Fact: VAR was first used in the FIFA World Cup in 2018 and has since been adopted in most top leagues.
