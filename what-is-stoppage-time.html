What is Stoppage Time in Football?

Stoppage time (also called injury time) is the additional time added to the end of each half of a football match to make up for time lost due to:

  • Injuries
  • Substitutions
  • Goal celebrations
  • Time-wasting or VAR reviews

The referee decides how much time to add based on input from the fourth official. It’s usually between 1 and 10 minutes depending on the events in the half.

Fun Fact: The longest recorded stoppage time in a professional match was over 28 minutes due to a major injury and extended VAR delays.
