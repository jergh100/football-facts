What Does Offside Mean in Football?

The offside rule is designed to prevent attackers from gaining an unfair advantage by hanging close to the opponent’s goal.

A player is in an offside position if:

  • They are nearer to the opponent’s goal line than both the ball and the second-last defender
  • They are involved in active play (receiving a pass or interfering with play)

A player is not offside if they are:

  • In their own half of the pitch
  • Level with the second-last defender
  • Receiving the ball from a goal kick, corner, or throw-in

Fun Fact: The offside rule has existed in some form since the 1800s — but it still sparks debate every week!
